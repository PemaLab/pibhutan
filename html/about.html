About Us

In the late 1980s and early 1990s, Bhutanese citizens of Nepali origin were subjected to a campaign of forced exile by the Bhutanese government. This was part of a larger process of cultural homogenization in the country, in which the Bhutanese government sought to preserve the dominant culture of the Ngalop people, who had absolute power in their hands.

The ethnic Nepalese, also known as Lhotshampas, are primarily of Nepali origin and make up a significant minority in Bhutan. They have a distinct culture and language and had lived in Bhutan for generations. However, in the 1980s and early 1990s, the Bhutanese government introduced a series of laws and policies aimed at promoting the dominant Ngalop culture, which ethnic Nepalese perceived as discriminatory.

As a result, many ethnic Nepalese became politically active and began to demand greater representation and rights. This led to a crackdown by the Bhutanese government, which responded with violence and forced exile. Over the next few years, tens of thousands of ethnic Nepalese were forcibly evicted from Bhutan and forced to flee to refugee camps in Nepal.

The situation of the Bhutanese refugees has been a source of ongoing concern and controversy, with many of them remaining in exile for over two decades. Efforts have been made to find a durable solution to the refugee crisis, but a lasting resolution has yet to be achieved. Currently, close to seven thousand Bhutanese refugees live in two refugee camps in Nepal without any foreign assistance. And a majority of them have been relocated to eight different western countries, including Canada, the United States, Denmark, the United Kingdom, the Netherlands, Australia, and Norway.

About PIB

The Peace Initiative Bhutan (PIB) is a non-profit organization based in the United States of America. Its primary goal is to promote positive peace and reconciliation in Bhutan through mutual trust and understanding. PIB does not have an adversarial approach toward the Government of Bhutan; instead, it seeks to find a mutually beneficial solution through sincere dialogue, compassionate listening, and active participation in a comprehensive peacebuilding process. It plays a positive role in bridging the existing gap between the Government of Bhutan and the Bhutanese in the diaspora.
