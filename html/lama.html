INTERN

Jyoma Lama Tamang

Jyoma Lama Tamang, a Political Science graduate from Temple University, brings a resolute commitment to community empowerment and grassroots initiatives. As a first-generation Nepali, Jyoma’s bachelor’s degree in political science serves as a testament to unwavering dedication for social change. The embodiment of passion for social justice and community engagement, Jyoma eagerly anticipates the opportunity to infuse these principles into the Peace Initiative Bhutan internship.

With active involvement in transformative endeavors such as IGNITE and the Poor People’s Economic Human Rights Campaign, Jyoma has cultivated a skill set harmonizing seamlessly with Peace Initiative Bhutan’s mission of cultivating peace, fostering healing, and advocating for justice. Jyoma’s background as a Field Organizer at CADC, LLC, coupled with the role of a Peer Career Advisor at the Salzberg Center, underscores a proven capacity for leadership, adept data analysis, and strategic communication acumen. These proficiencies stand as pivotal assets in guiding PIB’s campaign towards reconciliation.

Beyond the realm of professional pursuits, Jyoma’s artistic expression flourishes through mediums such as evocative paintings, captivating photography, and soul-stirring poetry. Anchored in a tapestry of diverse experiences, fortified by unyielding dedication, Jyoma is poised to make a substantial contribution to the aspirations of Peace Initiative Bhutan. Enthusiastically prepared to embark on a journey marked by collaboration, perpetual learning, and the generation of positive change, Jyoma is poised to effect enduring and impactful transformations.
