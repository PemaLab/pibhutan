FOUNDING MEMBER OF AAPI

Dr. Cora Quisumbing-King

Cora Quisumbing-King, of Cebu City, Philippines and Dover, New Hampshire earned her PhD in Social and Organizational Psychology from the University of Chicago where she was a Fulbright-Hays scholar. She is a founding member of the NH Racial Unity Team’s Asian American and Pacific Islander (AAPI) Advisory Group and serves on its Board. As a resident of Dover, Cora serves on its Committee for Racial Equity and Inclusion and was a past member of the McConnell Center Advisory Board, the Conservation Commission and two Master Plan Steering Committees. She has been a volunteer English tutor for many years at the Dover Adult Learning Center. Her advocacy includes indigenous rights, immigrant and refugee justice, the environment, and human rights in general.

Cora is Co-Chair of the NH AAPI Democrats and a member of the NHDP State Committee. She serves as the Convenor of the Social Justice Action Group of the Dover Democrats Executive Committee where she recently served as a member of its Executive Committee. Cora has written op-eds “My Turn: The Disappearing American Dream” (2020); “Being Who I Am” (2021) and “A Woman for the Ages” (2022). She maintains strong ties to her roots and is co-producer of three family books and acts as the chief genealogist of several clans.

In her professional life, Cora served as manager, consultant and facilitator in the field of organization & leadership development at Liberty Mutual, the Amoco Management Learning Center, the Federal Reserve Bank of Chicago and Banco Popular.
